Application of Baits and Gels

Cockroach baits and gels are a highly effective tool for controlling infestations. These products contain insecticides mixed with attractive food sources. Cockroaches consume the bait and carry it back to their hiding places, where it can be shared with other cockroaches, leading to a significant reduction in the population. Baits are particularly useful in areas where spraying liquid insecticides is not practical or desirable, such as around food preparation areas. We strategically place baits in areas where cockroaches are known to forage, ensuring they are easily accessible to the pests while remaining out of reach for children and pets. Gel baits can be applied in cracks and crevices, providing targeted treatment in areas where cockroaches hide. The active ingredients in these baits work by disrupting the cockroach's nervous system, leading to death. As cockroaches often engage in coprophagy and necrophagy (eating feces and dead bodies), the insecticide can spread throughout the population, creating a cascading effect that helps eliminate even hidden cockroaches. This chain reaction is a key advantage of using baits in a comprehensive treatment plan. Effective bait placement requires knowledge of cockroach behavior and preferred hiding spots, which our experienced technicians possess. We ensure baits are placed in discreet locations to minimize visual impact while maximizing effectiveness. Proper bait selection is also important, as different cockroach species may respond better to certain types of bait formulations. Egg Capsules (Oothecae)

Finding cockroach egg capsules, also known as oothecae, is a definitive sign of a cockroach infestation and indicates that the population is actively breeding. Oothecae are small, purse-shaped casings that contain multiple cockroach eggs. The appearance of the oothecae varies depending on the species. For example, German cockroach oothecae are typically light brown and elongated, containing 30-40 eggs, while American cockroach oothecae are darker and larger, containing around 16 eggs. Finding these capsules, especially multiple ones, means that new cockroaches are being born, and the infestation is likely to grow rapidly if not addressed. Female cockroaches often deposit their oothecae in protected locations, such as cracks, crevices, under furniture, or behind appliances, to shield them from predators and harsh environmental conditions. They may also carry the oothecae with them before depositing them shortly before the eggs hatch. Identifying and removing oothecae is an important part of effective cockroach control, as it helps to break the breeding cycle. German Cockroaches

The German cockroach (Blattella germanica) is one of the most common and troublesome indoor cockroach species found worldwide, including in Mooresville, NC.
